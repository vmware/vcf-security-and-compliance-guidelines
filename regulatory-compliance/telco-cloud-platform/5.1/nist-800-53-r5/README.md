# NIST SP 800-53 Revision 5

**NIST Special Publication 800-53 Revision 5: Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations** is the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology's comprehensive catalog of security and privacy controls. Released in September 2020 (with updates through December 2020), Revision 5 represents a significant evolution from earlier versions by decoupling the controls from a purely federal focus—making them applicable to **all types of organizations**, including private sector, critical infrastructure, and state/local governments. The publication organizes controls into **20 families** (e.g., Access Control, Audit and Accountability, Incident Response, Supply Chain Risk Management) and introduces a unified framework that integrates **privacy controls** alongside security controls for the first time. Organizations select and tailor controls using a risk-based approach, guided by companion publications such as *SP 800-53A* (assessment procedures) and *SP 800-53B* (control baselines for Low, Moderate, and High impact systems). NIST 800-53 R5 serves as a foundational reference for federal compliance under **FISMA** and underpins frameworks like the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** and **FedRAMP**, while also being widely adopted internationally as a benchmark for robust security and privacy governance.
